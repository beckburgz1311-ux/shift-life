# SHIFT//LIFE — Game Design

## Core fantasy

Every week is a run. Andy balances hospitality leadership, family, recovery, creativity, hobbies and long-term dreams while limited by energy and time.

The game is not about perfectly maximising every statistic. Strong play means choosing which costs are acceptable and building a life that remains worth living.

## Run structure

1. Choose a weekly intention.
2. Spend three activity slots per day for seven days.
3. React to random life events.
4. Defeat Saturday Night Service.
5. Receive an ending and permanent Perspective.

## Resources

- Energy: the immediate ability to act.
- Stress: accumulated pressure; high values threaten burnout.
- Family: connection with Becky, Isla and Quinn.
- Team morale: the Orange Tree team's confidence and independence.
- Creativity: progress toward music, THRESHOLD and future projects.
- Money: weekly breathing room and progress toward Japan.

## Design principles

- Humour should feel affectionate, specific and recognisable.
- Work success must not automatically equal life success.
- Family members are characters and companions, not resource dispensers.
- Delegation should usually outperform heroic overwork over multiple runs.
- Failure should reveal new story material rather than simply punish the player.
- Real people and workplaces should remain fictionalised and privacy-safe.
